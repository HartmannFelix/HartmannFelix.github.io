I am a PhD student (2015- ) in Political Science at the University of Gothenburg affiliated with the Program on Governance and Local Development. During the spring semester 2018, I was a Visiting PhD at the Center on the Politics of Development (CPD) at UC Berkeley.

My research is broadly focused on Comparative Politics and Political Economy in low and high income democracies. Especially, I am interested in how voters evaluate the performance and qualities of politicians, under which conditions politicians perform well, and how this influences political accountability and electoral selection.

My work employs a multi-method approach, using experimental, quasi-experimental, and observational data as well as qualitative fieldwork.
